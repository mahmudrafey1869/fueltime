Unlocking Precision with Bootstrapping

The Bootstrap method is a non-parametric approach to statistical inference. Because our sample size of 23 observations is relatively small, traditional Gaussian assumptions may not hold true.

By resampling our 23 data points 5,000 times with replacement, we simulate the sampling distribution of the mean. This allows us to calculate an empirical standard error and confidence intervals without assuming a normal distribution.
